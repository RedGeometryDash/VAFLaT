How do you say "he/she is normal" in Spanish?

The pronoun isn't usually required in Spanish.

“Es” in Spanish means “he/she/it is.” All words ending in -al in English (eg. normal) are the same in Spanish.

Complete the sentence:

"Normally it's important."

Many words ending with -ant in English are the same in Spanish but end with -ante. -mente in Spanish is equivalent to -ly in English (eg. normally → normalmente).

How do you say "I want confirmation" in Spanish?

The pronoun isn't usually required in Spanish.

"I want" in Spanish is "quiero." All words ending in -ation in English are the same in Spanish but end in -ación.

How do you say "I don't want to celebrate" in Spanish?

The pronoun isn't usually required in Spanish.

To make a verb negative you simply put "no" before it (eg. I don't want → no quiero). All -ación words can be turned into -ar verbs, just replace the -ación with -ar to get the unconjugated verb (eg. celebración → celebrar).
